> A Chinese cat-care encyclopedia and smart cat assistant for everyday cat owners. Explore cat breeds, beginner guides, daily care, product references, symptom triage, and practical Q&A in one place.

## What You Can Do

- Explore cat breeds: learn about appearance, personality, care difficulty, suitable owners, and common health concerns.
- Start from zero: follow step-by-step beginner guidance for preparation, settling in, feeding, vaccination, deworming, neutering, and daily care.
- Learn practical tips: browse advice on diet, litter boxes, cleaning, play, home setup, behavior issues, and everyday observation.
- Compare cat products: review common cat food, canned food, treats, supplements, and daily-use products with price ranges, pros, cons, and notes.
- Handle urgent signals: quickly understand the risk level of vomiting, diarrhea, urinary blockage, low energy, accidental ingestion, and other warning signs.
- Ask the smart assistant: use the assistant when your question is not covered by the site.

## Suggested Way To Use It

1. If this is your first cat, start with the beginner guide and follow the timeline for supplies, food, vet checks, and home safety.
2. If you are choosing a cat, open the breed library and compare coat length, body type, personality, and care needs.
3. For daily problems, search keywords such as food transition, soft stool, litter box, tooth brushing, or window safety.
4. Before buying products, check the product section for common brand information, pros, cons, and cautions.
5. If your cat suddenly looks unwell, open the emergency section first and judge whether immediate veterinary care is needed.
6. If the site does not cover your question, ask the smart cat assistant directly.

## Who It Is For

- People preparing for their first cat
- New cat owners who need a clear daily routine
- Cat owners who want a structured knowledge base
- Anyone comparing cat breeds or common cat products
- Anyone who needs quick symptom triage before deciding the next step

## Safety Note

Meow Meow is designed as a cat-care reference and decision aid. For emergencies, medication, dosage, diagnosis, prescriptions, chronic disease, kittens, pregnant cats, senior cats, or cats with existing health conditions, please consult a veterinarian or animal hospital.

If your cat cannot urinate, vomits repeatedly, has obvious breathing difficulty, seizures, suspected poisoning, serious trauma, extreme lethargy, or abnormal body temperature, do not wait for online advice. Seek veterinary care immediately.
